## From School SPG to Adult SPG

At JP Prashala, the SPG was a structured, long-term engagement with a chosen problem:
Economics, Energy, Psychology, Public Health, Environment, and others. Students didn't just
study the problem — they built proposals, connected with practitioners, and took the perspective
of change agents, not just observers.

The **Adult SPG** is the lifelong continuation of this practice.

For a JP alumnus who has now built professional expertise and life experience, the Adult SPG is:
- A chosen domain of contribution *beyond* job description and family responsibility
- An organized, sustained engagement (not occasional donation or goodwill)
- Pursued with Organizational Attitude — goal clarity, discipline, cooperation, service
- Reflective of the leader's *Svadharma* — where unique capability meets deep need

---

## The SPG Selection Framework

Choosing your Adult SPG is one of the most important leadership decisions a JP alumnus makes.
It answers the question: **"For what will I be remembered beyond my career?"**

Use these four lenses to identify your SPG:

### Lens 1: The Vivekananda Lens — Where is India's Pain?
What problem in India most needs motivated, organized intelligence?
(Education, health, governance, environment, arts, rural development, gender equality,
technology access, judicial reform, entrepreneurship, cultural preservation...)

### Lens 2: The Svadharma Lens — Where is Your Unique Fit?
Where does your professional expertise, personal experience, and natural strength
intersect with India's need? Your SPG is most powerful when it is *authentically yours* —
not chosen because it's popular, but because it calls from your deepest nature.

### Lens 3: The SPG Lens — What Futurology Problem Do You Choose?
If you were still in class 9 and had to choose a 10-year problem to work on —
knowing what you know now about India, the world, and your own capability —
what would you choose?

### Lens 4: The Moksha Lens — What Will Outlast You?
Not what you can achieve, but what you can *initiate and sustain beyond yourself*.
The SPG that becomes an institution, a movement, or a model that continues — that is Moksha-grade work.
