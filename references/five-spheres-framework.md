## Integral Life Architecture for the Prabodhini-Formed Leader

## Why Five Spheres, Not Work-Life Balance

The conventional "work-life balance" framing is inadequate for a JP alumnus. It suggests two
opposing forces to be negotiated. The JP tradition offers something richer: **integral development**
across all dimensions of a complete human life, each sphere informing and enriching the others.

The Five Spheres model draws from:
- Sri Aurobindo's Integral Yoga (physical, vital, mental, psychic, spiritual)
- JP's five developmental dimensions (physical, emotional, intellectual, social, spiritual)
- The Purusharthas (Dharma, Artha, Kama, Moksha)
- Positive Psychology's PERMA model (Seligman)
- William James's conception of the multi-dimensional self

## The Five Spheres

### 🔵 Sphere 1: ATMA — The Inner Self
*Sadhana, Svadhyaya, Spiritual Depth*

This is the root sphere. When it is strong, all others are fed. When neglected, all others eventually
hollow out regardless of external success.

**What this includes:**
- Daily Sadhana practice (meditation, pranayama, contemplative reading)
- Svadhyaya — rigorous self-study and honest self-examination
- Alignment with Svadharma — the felt sense of living one's unique calling
- Sthitaprajna cultivation — the work of inner equanimity

**Coaching questions:**
- "When did you last feel deeply connected to your purpose, not just your tasks?"
- "What is your Sadhana practice telling you that your schedule is ignoring?"
- "Who are you when no one is watching and nothing is being achieved?"

**Health indicators:** Sense of meaning, inner stillness, access to Viveka in difficulty

**Warning signs:** Chronic sense of emptiness despite external success, reactive rather than responsive
in stress, loss of connection to why any of it matters

---

### 🟡 Sphere 2: SANGHA — Family and Close Relationships
*Grihastha Dharma, Loving Presence, Relational Depth*

The Grihastha (householder) stage of life is not a compromise of spiritual aspiration.
In the Indian tradition, it is the forge where character is tested and refined daily.

**What this includes:**
- Spouse/partner relationship — quality of presence, not just time
- Children — being a formative influence, not just a provider
- Parents — honoring the lineage while establishing one's own
- Close friends — the Satsang of trusted peers who hold each other to truth

**Coaching questions:**
- "What is your family receiving from you — your presence or your leftover energy?"
- "When did you last have a conversation with your partner/child that was fully about them?"
- "Who in your inner circle tells you difficult truths? Are you listening?"

**Health indicators:** Felt sense of genuine connection; family members' wellbeing; presence in
conversations (not distracted)

**Warning signs:** Family relationships managed like projects; intimacy replaced by transactions;
children growing without knowing the real you; spouse carrying silent burden

---

### 🟠 Sphere 3: KARMA BHOOMI — Professional and Creative Contribution
*Svadharma in Action, Mastery, Economic Flourishing*

Work is not separate from spiritual life. For the JP alumnus, the professional domain is the
primary arena of *Karma Yoga* — of applying intelligence, skill, and character to produce
tangible value in the world.

**What this includes:**
- Professional mastery and continuous learning
- Economic self-sufficiency that funds freedom of contribution
- Creative expression through one's domain of expertise
- Leadership and mentoring of the next generation in one's field

**Coaching questions:**
- "Is your work an expression of your Svadharma, or are you performing someone else's Dharma for a salary?"
- "Who are you mentoring in your field? Who is receiving your mature knowledge?"
- "Are you building something that will outlast your tenure?"

**Health indicators:** Growth trajectory; sense of mastery; mentoring relationships; economic stability

**Warning signs:** Work feels like survival rather than contribution; no investment in learning; no
succession or mentoring; economic anxiety creating ethical compromise

---

### 🟢 Sphere 4: SAMAJ SEVA — National and Community Contribution
*The Adult SPG, Dharma-Sansthapana, Organized Action for India*

This is the sphere that distinguishes the JP alumnus from most professionals. JP embedded
the conviction that *individual achievement is incomplete without organized contribution to India*.

**The Adult SPG concept:**
Every JP alumnus should carry an active "Special Purpose Group" commitment — a chosen domain of
contribution that transcends their job: education, environment, governance, health, arts, rural
development, or any other field where India needs motivated intelligence.

**What this includes:**
- An active long-term engagement in a cause larger than personal career
- Organizational Attitude in practice — building, not just participating
- Political literacy and civic engagement
- National pride expressed through action, not just sentiment

**Coaching questions:**
- "What is your Adult SPG — the problem you have chosen to work on for India?"
- "Are you contributing organized effort, or just goodwill? What would make it 10x more effective?"
- "Where does your unique Prabodhini-formed intelligence serve India most distinctively?"

**Health indicators:** Active SPG with measurable progress; clarity about chosen contribution domain;
building with others, not alone

**Warning signs:** No active organized contribution beyond occasional donations; disconnection from
national issues; resignation ("Nothing will change"); contribution limited to forwarding WhatsApp messages

---

### 🔴 Sphere 5: SHARIRA-PRANA — Physical and Vital Energy
*Bio-Integrity, Life-Force, Sustainability of the Mission*

The ancient Indian wisdom is clear: a weakened *Sharira* (body) and depleted *Prana* (life-force)
cannot sustain either worldly achievement or spiritual development. The JP leader who neglects
physical health is mortgaging the future mission for present demands.

**What this includes:**
- Physical fitness (strength, endurance, flexibility appropriate to age)
- Sleep as sacred — not a variable to be optimized away
- Nutritional intelligence — food as *Prasad* (sacred sustenance), not just fuel
- Prana management — breath, movement, nature, rest
- Sensory and digital hygiene — protecting attention from fragmentation

**Coaching questions:**
- "Is your body a vehicle for the mission, or has it become a limitation?"
- "What does your energy level at 8 PM tell you about the design of your day?"
- "What would you need to change in your physical habits to serve for 20 more years?"

**Health indicators:** Sustained energy through the day; restful sleep; absence of chronic stress
conditions; physical capability for chosen activities

**Warning signs:** Persistent exhaustion; using stimulants to maintain pace; chronic conditions
worsening without intervention; body as afterthought

## The Integral Balance Audit

Rate each sphere (1–10) honestly, once per quarter:

| Sphere | Score | Thriving Signs | Neglect Signs |
|--------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Atma (Inner Self) | /10 | | |
| Sangha (Family/Friends) | /10 | | |
| Karma Bhoomi (Professional) | /10 | | |
| Samaj Seva (National/Community) | /10 | | |
| Sharira-Prana (Physical/Vital) | /10 | | |

**The JP Integration Principle**:
- No sphere should fall below 5 for more than one quarter without active intervention.
- The sphere that feels most uncomfortable to assess honestly is the one most in need of attention.
- A perfect 10 in one sphere cannot compensate for a 2 in another. Integration, not maximization.

## The Sphere Interaction Map

Each sphere feeds and can drain the others:

```
         ATMA (Root Sphere)
              ↕
    ┌─────────────────────┐
    ↕                     ↕
SANGHA ←──────────────→ SAMAJ SEVA
    ↕                     ↕
    └─────────────────────┘
              ↕
       KARMA BHOOMI
              ↕
       SHARIRA-PRANA
```

**Cross-sphere coaching principle**: When a leader is stuck in one sphere, often the solution
lies in *another sphere*. Professional burnout may be resolved through Atma strengthening.
Family conflict may be resolved through Samaj Seva that gives the leader meaning beyond home.

## The Purusharthas as Sphere-to-Sphere Bridge

The Four Purusharthas map naturally to the Five Spheres:

| Purushartha | Primary Sphere | How It Bridges |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Dharma** (Right Action) | Atma | Governs all decisions across spheres |
| **Artha** (Prosperity) | Karma Bhoomi | Enables freedom in all other spheres |
| **Kama** (Fulfilment) | Sangha + Sharira | Joy that sustains long-term Dharma |
| **Moksha** (Liberation) | Atma + Samaj Seva | Freedom that comes from non-attached service |
