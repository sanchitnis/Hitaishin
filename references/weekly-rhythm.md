# The Weekly Svadhyaya Rhythm
## A Seven-Day Architecture for Integral Living

---

> *"Svadhyaya"* — Self-study; the practice of ongoing, honest self-examination.
> One of the Niyamas of Patanjali's Yoga Sutras. JP's foundational discipline made adult.

---

## The Weekly Architecture

| Day | Primary Sphere | Theme | Key Practice |
|-----|---------------|-------|-------------|
| **Sunday** | Atma | Svadhyaya & Week Design | Weekly Review (60–90 min) |
| **Monday** | Karma Bhoomi | Launch & Focus | Week's primary professional intention set |
| **Tuesday** | Karma Bhoomi + Samaj Seva | Deep Work + SPG | Protected deep work; SPG advance |
| **Wednesday** | Sangha | Relational Presence | Intentional family/friend time |
| **Thursday** | Samaj Seva + Karma Bhoomi | Mentoring + Impact | Mentoring sessions; community work |
| **Friday** | Karma Bhoomi | Closure & Gratitude | Complete; handover; appreciate the week |
| **Saturday** | Sharira-Prana + Sangha | Rest & Renewal | No primary professional work; body and connection |

---

## 🌿 SUNDAY: The Weekly Svadhyaya Review (60–90 minutes)

*This is the most important hour of the week. Protect it absolutely.*

### Step 1: The Five Spheres Scan (15 min)
Score each sphere (1–10) for the past week. Write one sentence of honest assessment for each:

| Sphere | Score | This Week's Honest Truth |
|--------|-------|--------------------------|
| Atma (Inner Self) | | |
| Sangha (Family/Friends) | | |
| Karma Bhoomi (Professional) | | |
| Samaj Seva (National/Community) | | |
| Sharira-Prana (Physical/Vital) | | |

**The JP Svadhyaya principle**: Don't rationalize. Don't compare to others. Just see clearly.

**After scoring**: Copy this week's five scores (+ integral average) into the **Five Spheres History** table in your `profiles/[name]-profile.md`. This takes 2 minutes and creates longitudinal coaching data.

### Step 2: The Sthitaprajna Reflection (10 min)
- Where did I react rather than respond this week?
- Where did I hold equanimity in a difficult situation — and what enabled it?
- What would the "Witness" in me say about how this week was lived?

### Step 2A: The Dopamine and Happiness Audit (5-10 min)
- What drove my effort this week most often: meaning/mastery/contribution, or urgency/novelty/validation?
- Did I experience at least three non-work joy/recovery moments this week?
- Which sphere was reward-starved (Atma, Sangha, Karma Bhoomi, Samaj Seva, or Sharira-Prana)?
- What one environmental change will reduce distraction and improve calm motivation next week?

### Step 3: The Organizational Attitude Check (10 min)
Vivekananda's five components — honest self-assessment:

| Component | Was This Present This Week? | Evidence |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------|
| Lakshya Nishtha (Goal commitment) | | |
| Anushasan (Self-discipline) | | |
| Sahakar (Cooperative spirit) | | |
| Seva Bhava (Service orientation) | | |
| Sanghatan (Organization-building) | | |

### Step 4: The SPG Pulse (5 min)
- Did I advance my chosen Adult SPG contribution this week, even in a small way?
- What is the next specific action for my SPG in the coming week?
- Am I building with others, or contributing alone?

### Step 5: The Mentor's Mirror (5 min)
- Who did I mentor or develop this week?
- Who offered me wisdom I needed — did I receive it?
- Am I in a Satsang — a community of peers who speak truth to each other?

### Step 6: Next Week's Integral Sankalpa (15 min)

Set one intentional commitment per sphere for the coming week.
Use the canonical Sankalpa format and criteria defined in `agents/accountability-partner.md` → **Step 3: Design This Week's Sankalpas**.

*(Format reproduced here for standalone use):*

```
Atma Sankalpa: This week I commit to _______ in my inner life.
Sangha Sankalpa: This week I will give genuine presence to _______ .
Karma Bhoomi Sankalpa: This week's primary professional contribution will be _______ .
Samaj Seva Sankalpa: This week I will advance my SPG by _______ .
Sharira-Prana Sankalpa: This week I will honor my body by _______ .
```

### Step 7: The Week's Learning Distillation (10 min)
- What is the one insight from this week that I want to carry forward permanently?
- Is this worth writing? (If yes — write a paragraph. This becomes the Prabodhini Corpus.)
- Who would benefit from this insight? Can I share it without ego-investment?

## 🚀 MONDAY: The Week Launch (20 min)

*Clarity precedes commitment. Start the week with intention, not inbox.*

1. Re-read Sunday's Integral Sankalpa (2 min)
2. Review the week's calendar — classify each commitment by sphere (3 min)
3. Identify the ONE most important professional contribution of this week (2 min)
4. Identify the ONE relationship that most needs attention this week (2 min)
5. Set the Deep Work block for Tuesday (calendar block if not already done) (2 min)
6. Recite the Week's Sankalpa — aloud or in writing (2 min)

---

## 🔷 TUESDAY: The Deep Work + SPG Day

*Protected from institutional gravity. Dedicated to what only you can do.*

**Morning**: Protected deep work — no meetings before noon.
**Afternoon (where possible)**: SPG contribution time — the organized effort for India.

The Tuesday discipline trains the most important leadership capacity: **prioritizing the significant
over the urgent**. The urgent will always crowd out the significant unless you have a structural
commitment that the significant comes first.

---

## 🌿 WEDNESDAY: The Relational Presence Day

*Connection is not a reward for completing work. It is the work.*

One non-negotiable for Wednesday:
- A fully present, unhurried conversation with a family member — with no hidden agenda
- At least one mentoring exchange with someone in your field or community

**The JP mentoring principle**: The leader who received the gift of JP education carries a debt
to the next generation. Not out of obligation, but from the fullness of gratitude.

---

## 🟢 THURSDAY: The Mentoring + Community Day

*Organizational Attitude in practice — building with others.*

- Mentoring sessions (junior colleagues, students, community members)
- Team alignment — enabling others to act independently of you
- SPG meeting or community engagement

**The Coaching Question for Thursday**: "Who will function better next week because of a
conversation I had today?"

---

## 🏁 FRIDAY: The Closure and Gratitude Day

*Wisdom completes what it begins.*

- Close open loops from the week (or consciously decide to carry them to next week)
- Send one genuine appreciation — not generic, but specific to what someone did this week
- Write one paragraph: "This week I contributed ______. This week I learned ______."
- Hand over anything that does not need to travel with you into the weekend

**The JP closing practice**: The school trained students in honest self-assessment.
Friday is the adult practice — assessing the week without defensiveness, without self-flagellation,
with the calm curiosity of a scientist observing data.

---

## ⛰️ SATURDAY: The Renewal Day

*Even the Maharshi rested. Rest is the Sadhana between Sadhanas.*

- No primary professional work (emergencies excepted, not habitual)
- Physical activity that is chosen for joy, not performance
- Time in nature (JP's connection to Paristhiti — the living environment)
- Creative activity: music, art, cooking, craft — anything that is purely alive
- Family time where presence is the entire agenda

**The PERMA principle for Saturday**: The *Positive Emotions* and *Relationships* elements of
flourishing require input, not just outputs. Saturday is where that input happens.
