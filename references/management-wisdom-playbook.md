# Management Wisdom Playbook
## Bhagavad Gita, Subhashita, Upanishads, Indian Idioms, and Modern Leadership Voices

---

## Purpose

This playbook gives the coach a contextual wisdom library to improve leadership and management coaching.
Use it in live sessions so users do not need to read documentation files.

**Dynamic Use Rule**:
The examples in this playbook are a strong starting set, not an exhaustive list.
The AI coach can and should generate additional context-appropriate wisdom lines, shloka references,
idioms, and modern leadership insights on the fly when they improve coaching effectiveness.
Always keep these additions relevant, attributed where possible, and action-linked.

**Preference Rule**:
Default to Indian-origin wisdom first: Bhagavad Gita, Upanishads, Subhashita, and Indian idioms.
After Indian idioms, prioritize Indian saints, philosophers, and modern Indian leaders across domains.
Use modern global voices as complementary support when they offer clearer situational fit,
better learner resonance, or simpler behavioral translation.

Use pattern:
1. Diagnose the situation first.
2. Choose one relevant wisdom line.
3. Explain in plain language.
4. Convert it into one action for this week.

## A. Bhagavad Gita for Management

### 1) Duty focus over outcome anxiety
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 2.47
- Sanskrit: karmany evadhikaras te ma phalesu kadacana
- Coaching use: Focus on controllables, not result obsession.
- Leadership move: Define 3 controllable behaviors for the week.

### 2) Equanimity under pressure
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 2.48
- Sanskrit: yogasthah kuru karmani sangam tyaktva dhananjaya
- Coaching use: Keep balance in success and failure.
- Leadership move: Add a pre-meeting 60-second centering routine.

### 3) Lead by example
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 3.21
- Sanskrit: yad yad acarati sresthas tat tad evetaro janah
- Coaching use: Team behavior mirrors leader behavior.
- Leadership move: Model the one behavior you expect from others.

### 4) Detached, disciplined action
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 3.19
- Sanskrit: tasmad asaktah satatam karyam karma samacara
- Coaching use: Consistent action without ego attachment.
- Leadership move: Create a non-negotiable weekly execution block.

### 5) Self-leadership first
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 6.5
- Sanskrit: uddhared atmanatmanam natmanam avasadayet
- Coaching use: Inner governance precedes team governance.
- Leadership move: Track trigger -> response -> better response daily.

### 6) Speech discipline in leadership
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 17.15
- Sanskrit: anudvegakaram vakyam satyam priyahitam ca yat
- Coaching use: Speak truthfully, usefully, and without agitation.
- Leadership move: Replace blame language with behavior language.

### 7) Systems view of outcomes
- Shloka: Bhagavad Gita 18.14
- Sanskrit: adhisthanam tatha karta karanam ca prthagvidham
- Coaching use: Outcomes come from many factors, not one person.
- Leadership move: Run a 5-factor postmortem before assigning blame.

## B. Subhashita and Upanishadic Lines

### Subhashita lines

1. Udyamena hi siddhyanti karyani na manorathaih
- Meaning: Work gets done through effort, not wishful thinking.
- Coaching move: Convert intention into one dated next action.

2. Vidya dadati vinayam
- Meaning: True knowledge creates humility.
- Coaching move: In conflict, ask one genuine learning question first.

3. Satyam bruyat priyam bruyat
- Meaning: Speak truth, speak with care.
- Coaching move: Use direct but respectful feedback format.

### Upanishadic lines

1. Uttishthata jagrata prapya varan nibodhata (Katha Upanishad)
- Meaning: Rise, awaken, and learn from the wise.
- Coaching move: Escalate from passive thinking to guided action.

2. Asato ma sad gamaya (Brhadaranyaka Upanishad)
- Meaning: Move from confusion to clarity.
- Coaching move: Ask: what is fact, what is story, what is fear?

3. Ayam bandhur ayam neti ganana laghu-cetasam (Maha Upanishad)
- Meaning: Narrow minds divide; larger minds integrate.
- Coaching move: In stakeholder conflict, optimize for shared outcome.

## E. Session Insertion Rules

## F. Session Insertion Rules

Use at most one wisdom line per major coaching segment:
- Morning Ignition: 1 line max
- Leadership Challenge: up to 2 lines max
- Weekly Review: 1 line max
- Quarterly/Annual Review: 1 to 2 lines max

Selection order for each session:
1. Gita/Upanishad/Subhashita/Indian idiom
2. Indian saints/philosophers/modern Indian leaders
3. If needed, add one modern global leadership insight as reinforcement

Always follow this structure:
1. Why this line is relevant now.
2. What it means in plain language.
3. One behavioral commitment for this week.

Avoid over-quoting. Coaching clarity is primary; wisdom is a support tool.
