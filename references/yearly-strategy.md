# The Annual Vision Retreat
## The Prabodhini Leader's Year-End and Year-Begin Practice

---

> *"Nana Pari Prayas Karawa"* — Attempt in many ways. When one path closes, the mission doesn't.
> — Samarth Ramdas, Dasabodha

---

## Purpose

The Annual Vision Retreat is a 1–2 day personal offsite — ideally in nature, away from normal
environment — conducted once a year (December-January or on personal new year / birthday).

It is not a goal-setting exercise. It is a **recalibration of the whole person**:
Who am I becoming? Am I living the life I chose, or the life that happened to me?
Is my Svadharma still the same, or have I evolved?

---

## Before the Retreat: The Pre-Work (One Week Prior)

Collect and review the following:

1. **Weekly Svadhyaya Reviews** from the past year — what patterns emerge?
2. **Five Spheres quarterly scores** — which sphere declined most? Which grew?
3. **SPG Progress** — what did I actually accomplish in my Adult SPG this year?
4. **Mentoring record** — who did I invest in? What did they achieve?
5. **Learning log** — what new understanding did this year bring?
6. **One honest letter from a trusted person** who can speak truth about your shadow side

---

## Day 1: The Honest Retrospective

### Morning: The Year as a Whole

**Opening Sankalpa**: Light a lamp or sit in nature. Set the intention for radical honesty,
not performance.

**The JP Year Audit** — five sections:

#### 1. The Five Spheres Retrospective
For each sphere, answer in writing (not just thinking):
- What were the three defining moments in this sphere this year?
- Where did I show courage?
- Where did I fail to show up as who I know myself to be?
- What did I learn about this sphere that I didn't know last year?

#### 2. The Organizational Attitude Year Review
*Apply Vivekananda's five-component framework to the full year:*
- **Lakshya Nishtha**: Was my goal commitment sustained, or did I waver and revise for convenience?
- **Anushasan**: Where was I self-disciplined, and where did I break my own standards?
- **Sahakar**: Did I build true cooperative relationships, or compete disguised as collaborating?
- **Seva Bhava**: Was my work fundamentally oriented toward others, or toward my recognition?
- **Sanghatan**: What organization, system, or institution did I build that didn't exist last year?

#### 3. The Sthitaprajna Self-Assessment
- What was the hardest moment of this year?
- How did I respond — as a Sthitaprajna, or as a reactive ego?
- What would my response have been five years ago? How have I grown?
- Where am I still reactive in ways that don't serve anyone?

#### 4. The Relationships Inventory
*Without naming blame, assess:*
- Which relationships grew in depth and trust this year?
- Which became more transactional and less genuine?
- Who deserves an acknowledgment I haven't given?
- Who did I fail to be present for?
- Who offered me wisdom I resisted receiving?

#### 5. The Learning Harvest
- What three insights, if I embodied them fully, would change everything?
- What book, conversation, or experience most shifted my understanding?
- What question am I carrying that I haven't yet found the answer to?

### Afternoon: The Shadow Work

*The JP alumnus was trained in intellectual rigor. Apply this to the self.*

**The Immunity to Change Map** (Kegan & Lahey):
Choose one behavior you've wanted to change for more than a year but haven't:

```
1. The commitment: I want to _____ .
2. What I'm doing instead: Despite this, I continue to _____ .
3. The competing commitment: I am also committed to _____ (the hidden agenda).
4. The Big Assumption: I believe that if I change, _____ will happen (the fear beneath).
```

Sit with this. It is not a problem to solve today. It is a truth to acknowledge.

### Evening: The Learning Letter

Write a letter to yourself dated five years from now.
Begin: *"In 2030, looking back at 2025, what I most wanted you to know was..."*

## Day 2: The Prospective — Vision and Commitment

### Morning: The Svadharma Recalibration

*Svadharma is not static. Life experience evolves it. Check in annually.*

**Three questions** (answer in writing, not bulleting — full paragraphs):

1. *"What is the highest use of my life at this stage?"*
   Not what is expected, not what I've always done — what does this moment call for?

2. *"If I had 10 more years of prime contribution ahead, what would I choose to build?"*
   Let this be honest, not modest.

3. *"What am I holding onto that is no longer truly mine to carry?"*
   What role, commitment, or identity am I maintaining out of inertia, fear, or obligation —
   that is actually blocking the next chapter?

### Late Morning: The 10-Year Vision

*The SPG Futurology instinct — think long.*

For each sphere, write a vivid, honest answer:

```
In 10 years (by [year]):

ATMA: My inner life looks like _______ .
      The Sadhana I am practicing is _______ .
      The quality of my consciousness is _______ .

SANGHA: My closest relationships look like _______ .
         My children/family are _______ .
         The kind of friend/spouse/parent I am is _______ .

KARMA BHOOMI: My professional/creative contribution is _______ .
               What I have built that will outlast me is _______ .

SAMAJ SEVA: My Adult SPG has achieved _______ .
             India is different because _______ .
             The leaders I have catalyzed are now _______ .

SHARIRA-PRANA: My body is _______ .
                My energy and vitality allow me to _______ .
```

### Afternoon: The Annual Integral Sankalpa

Set *three* Annual Sankalpas — not goals, but *commitments of being*:

```
SANKALPA 1 (Atma or Sangha): 
"This year, I commit to becoming _______ in my inner/relational life."

SANKALPA 2 (Karma Bhoomi or Samaj Seva):
"This year, I commit to building/contributing _______ in the world."

SANKALPA 3 (Integration):
"This year, the quality that I will most consciously cultivate is _______ ,
because it is the foundation everything else depends on."
```

### Evening: The Closing Ceremony

1. **Read your Learning Letter** from Day 1 evening aloud to yourself.
2. **Name one person** you will call or write in the first week to share a key insight.
3. **Set one concrete structural change** for the coming year (a time block, a practice,
   a relationship investment, an SPG commitment).
4. **The Vivekananda Closing Reflection**:
   *"Arise, Awake, and stop not until the goal is reached."*
   What is YOUR goal? Say it plainly.
5. **Silence**: Sit for 15 minutes in gratitude and stillness. The retreat ends in quiet.
