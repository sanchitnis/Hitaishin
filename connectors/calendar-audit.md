# Calendar Audit — Google Calendar Connector
## Aligning Time Allocation with the Five Spheres and Svadharma

---

> *"Your calendar is the truest autobiography of your values."*
> — Applied Paristhiti Jnana to personal time

---

## Purpose

The Calendar Audit converts Svadhyaya (self-study) from theory into data.
Most leaders believe they are living their values. The calendar often tells a different story.
This connector bridges declared priorities and actual time investment.

**Connector**: Google Calendar MCP (when active in session)

---

## The Five-Sphere Time Audit

When the Google Calendar connector is active, pull the past week's events and classify each by sphere:

| Sphere | Classification Criteria |
|--------|------------------------|
| 🔵 **Atma** | Meditation, Sadhana, Svadhyaya sessions, spiritual practice, philosophical study |
| 🟡 **Sangha** | Family meals (present, not working), friend time, couple time, children's events |
| 🟠 **Karma Bhoomi** | Professional meetings, deep work, creative work, learning in domain |
| 🟢 **Samaj Seva** | SPG meetings, community work, mentoring (outside professional role), civic engagement |
| 🔴 **Sharira-Prana** | Exercise, nature walks, meal preparation (mindful), sleep preparation time |
| ⚫ **Residual** | Commute, admin, passive scrolling, transition time |

## The Meeting Quality Audit

Not all time is equal. Within Karma Bhoomi hours especially, assess meeting quality:

For each meeting, classify:

| Type | Criteria | Coaching Action |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 🟢 **Generative** | Creates new value, insight, or organizational capacity | Protect and optimize |
| 🟡 **Maintenance** | Necessary for coordination; not creating new value | Shorten; automate where possible |
| 🔴 **Consuming** | Drains energy without proportional output; could be handled differently | Reduce, delegate, or redesign |
| ⚪ **Unclear** | No clear purpose or outcome defined | Never attend without an agenda |

**Meeting Health Score**: % of Karma Bhoomi meetings that are Generative
Target: >40% Generative

---

## The Organizational Attitude Meeting Check

For each meeting in the Samaj Seva or leadership category, assess:

Did this meeting advance any of the Organizational Attitude components?

| Organizational Attitude Component | Was it present? |
|-------------|----------------|
| Lakshya Nishtha (goal clarity advanced) | Y/N |
| Anushasan (accountability demonstrated) | Y/N |
| Sahakar (genuine cooperation happened) | Y/N |
| Seva Bhava (beneficiary focus maintained) | Y/N |
| Sanghatan (organization was strengthened) | Y/N |

If fewer than 3 were present: the meeting may be worth redesigning.

## Common Calendar Patterns in JP Alumni

| Pattern | What It Reveals | Coaching Response |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Atma = 0 hours** | Morning has been surrendered to others' agendas | Protect Zone 1 as sacred; it is not negotiable |
| **Sangha = logistics only** | Family time is task management, not presence | Schedule presence moments, not just events |
| **Karma Bhoomi = 80% meetings** | Deep work has been crowded out by coordination | Protect Tuesday morning absolutely; no meetings before noon |
| **Samaj Seva = 0** | National contribution is aspirational, not operational | Schedule the minimum viable SPG commitment |
| **No white space** | The schedule is a cage, not a container | Deliberate recovery time is not laziness — it is Prana |
| **Evenings filled with work** | Digital Sunset isn't happening | Boundary 8:30 PM; non-negotiable |
