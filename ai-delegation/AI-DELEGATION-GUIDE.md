## THE CORE INNOVATION

Traditional GTD says: delegate to humans what humans can do better.
The Prabodhini AI-Augmented GTD adds: **delegate to AI agents what AI can do well**,
so the leader's finite human energy is reserved for what only a human — specifically this
particular Prabodhini-formed human — can uniquely do.

This is not about efficiency. It is about **Svadharma at scale**.

Every hour saved from research, drafting, organising, and tracking is an hour available for:
- Deep thinking and wisdom-generation
- Genuine human connection (Sangha)
- Contemplative practice (Atma)
- Organised national contribution (Samaj Seva)
- The mentoring conversation that can't be automated

## HOW TO TRIGGER AN AI AGENT

In any GTD or coaching context, use this syntax to create an AI-delegated task:

```markdown
- [ ] [AI-DELEGATE] [Clear task description with expected output]
      Agent: [Agent name] | Priority: [H/M/L] | By: [DATE]
      Output format: [Summary / Draft / Plan / Analysis / List]
      Context: [Any background the agent needs]
      #ai-delegate #[sphere]
```

**Example:**
```markdown
- [ ] [AI-DELEGATE] Research top 5 models of gifted education globally and their 
      outcomes — with emphasis on India-applicable frameworks.
      Agent: Research Agent | Priority: M | By: 2026-05-10
      Output format: 3-page synthesis with table of models
      Context: For SPG proposal to AICTE on gifted education policy
      #ai-delegate #samaj
```

## THE AI DELEGATION DECISION MATRIX

Before delegating to an AI agent, ask:

| Question | If YES | If NO |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Does this require my personal wisdom, relationships, or values judgement? | Do it yourself | AI can handle |
| Would a reasonable output from AI require significant human editing to be usable? | Human-first, AI-assist | AI-first, human-review |
| Is this primarily information gathering, synthesis, or structuring? | Strong AI delegate | Consider human |
| Does this require creative originality uniquely mine? | Human | AI can scaffold |
| Would I be comfortable showing the AI output with minor edits? | Delegate | Co-create |

## QUALITY ASSURANCE PRINCIPLE

AI agents produce **first drafts**, **research scaffolds**, and **structured options** —
not final outputs that go to the world unreviewed.

Every AI output must pass through:
1. **Factual review**: Is the information accurate and current?
2. **Values review**: Does this reflect my authentic voice and Prabodhini values?
3. **Purpose review**: Does this advance the right Sankalpa and sphere?
4. **Human touch**: What needs to be added that only I can add?

Maintain this live log in the Waiting For list (`gtd/04-waiting-for.md`):

```markdown
| Task | Agent | Given | Expected | Received | Quality | Action |
|------|-------|-------|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| [Task] | Research | [DATE] | [DATE] | [DATE] | /5 | [Done/Editing/Rejected] |
```

**Monthly AI Delegation Review** (part of Monthly Compass):
- Which agent is most used? Is that appropriate?
- Which delegated tasks had highest quality output?
- Which required the most rework? Why?
- What new task categories could be delegated?
- Are AI agents freeing up more time for Atma, Sangha, and SPG — or just enabling more Karma Bhoomi work?

## ETHICAL PRINCIPLES FOR AI DELEGATION

The Prabodhini tradition's scientific temper and value of honesty applies to AI use:

1. **Transparency**: Never present AI-drafted content as if personally researched without disclosure where relevant
2. **Accountability**: You remain fully responsible for every output bearing your name, regardless of AI assistance
3. **Integrity**: Do not use AI to create the *appearance* of work that wasn't actually thought through
4. **Depth**: AI speeds the surface. The depth — the wisdom, the original insight — must come from you
5. **Non-attachment**: If AI produces something better than you would have — be genuinely glad. Ego has no place here.
