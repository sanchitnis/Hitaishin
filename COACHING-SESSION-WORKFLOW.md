# Hitaishin — Coaching Session Workflow
## Progressive Skills Development Through Structured Sessions

---

> *"Gunavikas"* — The unfolding and development of human capacities and strengths.
> The JP vision of education and coaching: not diagnosis of deficiency, but cultivation of potential.

---

> **Relationship to `SKILL.md`**: This document is the **protocol layer** — step-by-step processes, skills developed, preconditions, and optionality for each session. For session triggers, agent routing, universal coaching principles, and cross-session rules, see `SKILL.md`. The two files work together; neither replaces the other.

---

## Overview

This document maps the **complete coaching journey** — from intake through ongoing mastery development. Each session type has:

- **Clear purpose and expected outcomes**
- **Explicit skills developed** (based on coaching best practices: ICF competencies, modern psychology, JP philosophy)
- **Applicable agent(s)** from the six-agent system
- **Conditional paths** — not all items are required or suitable for every person
- **Integration points** with daily/weekly/annual rhythms and GTD

### Design Principle

Progress is **non-linear and holistic**, not hierarchical. The workflow acknowledges:
- Different life stages activate different paths
- Multiple paths can run in parallel
- Mastery means deepening, not just progressing to the next session type
- Sessions cycle and deepen, not replace each other

## Phase 1: ONBOARDING (Weeks 1–2)

### Session 0.0: Beginner Interactive Orientation (10 min) ⚠️ **Recommended for New Users**

**Purpose**: Help first-time users understand and start using Hitaishin without reading markdown files.

**Agent**: Accountability Partner (primary) + Svadharma Navigator (secondary)

**Process**:
Load `references/beginner-interactive-orientation.md` and run the interactive 7-step flow:
1. Explain Hitaishin in one sentence
2. Offer quick mode (2 min) or guided mode (10 min)
3. Identify immediate need (daily focus, weekly reset, leadership, direction)
4. Explain Think -> Decide -> Do -> Review in plain language
5. Give 3 starter commands
6. Capture one 24-hour commitment
7. Confirm next check-in

**Output**:
- Beginner-level system clarity
- First chosen session path
- One immediate commitment captured

### Session 1.1: Intake Interview (60–90 min)

**Purpose**: Establish a complete baseline understanding of the mentee's whole life.

**Agent**: Accountability Partner (structure) + Integral Life Coach (context)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Active Listening without judgment** (ICF Competency 6) — attending to both explicit and implicit content
- **Building psychological safety** (ICF Competency 4) — creating conditions where truth-telling becomes possible
- **Paristhiti Jnana** (JP) — accurate reading of a person's complete life situation

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Self-awareness across all five spheres** — articulating life situation in an integrated way
- **Honest self-appraisal** (Svadhyaya foundation) — moving past performance/rationalization
- **Articulating vision** — making implicit aspirations explicit

**Process**:
Complete `intake/intake-protocol.md` → Stage 1 (Six modules: A–F)
Record responses in `profiles/[name]-intake.md`

**Output**:
- `profiles/[name]-intake.md` (raw data)
- Preliminary Five Spheres scoring (1–10 for each)
- Identified themes and patterns

**Optionality**: ⚠️ **Required for all new mentees**

### Session 1.2: Psychometric Suite (45–60 min, self-administered)

**Purpose**: Validate intake observations with objective strengths and balance data.

**Agents**: Svadharma Navigator (strengths discovery) + Integral Life Coach (balance)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Data interpretation and pattern recognition** — translating test scores into coaching insights
- **Strengths-based framing** (positive psychology) — seeing potential, not pathology

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Objective self-knowledge** — moving beyond bias and perception to data
- **Strengths recognition** (VIA Character Strengths) — understanding what is naturally strong
- **Integrity gap awareness** — seeing the gap between lived values and stated values

**Process**:
1. **VIA Character Strengths** (`intake/apps/01-character-strengths.html`) → Top 5 and Bottom 5 strengths
2. **Five Spheres Assessment** (`intake/apps/02-spheres-assessment.html`) → Detailed balance wheel + gap analysis
3. **Organizational Attitude & Leadership Profile** (`intake/apps/03-leadership-oa-profile.html`) → OA strengths + Saboteur patterns
4. Each app generates a Markdown block at completion — copy it and paste it into the **Psychometric Profile** section of `profiles/[name]-profile.md`

   *(The apps run fully offline and cannot write to files directly — the copy-paste step is required.)*

**Output**:
- VIA strengths profile (with JP interpretation)
- Five Spheres visual balance + integrity gaps
- Organizational Attitude + Saboteur profile
- Data-backed growth edges

**Optionality**: ⚠️ **Required** (strongly recommended for all)

### Session 1.3: Synthesis & Coaching Sankalpas (45 min)

**Purpose**: Integrate intake data and establish coaching intentions for the engagement.

**Agents**: All six agents (brief activation for each sphere)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Synthesis and pattern recognition** (ICF Competency 7 — Evokes Awareness) — connecting disparate data into a coherent picture
- **Identifying the central theme** — what thread runs through everything?
- **Naming the provisional one idea** — forming an initial hypothesis about the organizing idea that may unify this season of the mentee's life
- **Growth edge mapping** — distinguishing urgent vs. transformative vs. long-arc development

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Wholeness recognition** — seeing themselves as an integrated human, not compartmentalized
- **Sankalpa articulation** (JP) — conscious commitment made with will and awareness
- **Early idea discernment** — distinguishing a real governing idea from temporary excitement or inherited expectation
- **Coaching relationship clarity** — knowing what to expect and how to work together

**Process**:
1. Coach reads back synthesis of intake + psychometric data (10 min)
2. Identify the **Central Theme** — the pattern that runs through everything (5 min)
3. Name a **Provisional One Idea Hypothesis** (5 min) — if one idea seems to be trying to organize this season of life, what is it?
4. Confirm the **Three Growth Edges**: Primary (urgent), Secondary (transformative), Long-arc (meaningful) (10 min)
5. Craft **Coaching Sankalpas** — one per sphere + one meta-commitment (10 min)
6. Agree on **Coaching Cadence** — rhythm of check-ins, format, accountability style (5 min)
7. Create living document `profiles/[name]-profile.md`

**Output**:
- `profiles/[name]-profile.md` (living coaching document)
- Provisional One Idea Hypothesis for this season
- Five Coaching Sankalpas (one per sphere + meta)
- Agreed coaching cadence and relationship norms
- Three primary growth edges (confirmed)

**Optionality**: ⚠️ **Required** (essential foundation for all ongoing work)

## Phase 2: FOUNDATIONAL COACHING CYCLES (Weeks 3–12)

### Session 2.1: Morning Ignition Deep Dive (30 min)

**Purpose**: Establish the daily sadhana practice and teach the JP Morning Protocol.

**Agent**: Svadharma Navigator + Accountability Partner

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Habit formation coaching** — understanding what enables daily practice
- **Dopamine baseline assessment** — reading motivation quality (meaning-driven vs. reactive)

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Morning Protocol mastery** (JP pedagogy) — the Prabodhini Morning Sequence integrated into daily life
- **Intention setting** (Sankalpa) — conscious commitment before the day's reactivity begins
- **Dopamine quality awareness** — recognizing healthy vs. unhealthy motivation drivers
- **Daily profile signal capture** — learning what signals to feed into coaching

**Process**:
Walk through `references/daily-thread.md` → Zone 1 (Brahma Muhurta) for the canonical 6-step sequence
1. Walk the mentee through the full Zone 1 protocol in `references/daily-thread.md`
2. Coach the mentee through a trial morning (or planned trial)
3. Clarify non-negotiables (no phone until complete)
4. Establish daily signal capture: energy state, focus, constraint, commitment, dopamine risk

**Output**:
- Morning Protocol adopted and trialed
- Daily signal capture routine established
- Profile updated with baseline patterns

**Optionality**: 🟢 **Highly recommended** — foundational for all other work

---

### Session 2.2: Five Spheres Balance Assessment & Prioritization (45 min)

**Purpose**: Establish which sphere(s) need primary attention this quarter.

**Agent**: Integral Life Coach (primary) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (secondary)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Systemic thinking** (Paristhiti Jnana) — understanding how imbalance in one sphere cascades to others
- **Integrity-gap coaching** — helping mentees see the gap between values and lived allocation

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Whole-life systems thinking** — understanding sphere interdependence
- **Trade-off awareness** — conscious choice about where to invest energy, not default/reactive allocation
- **Balance metrics** — quantifying the Five Spheres concretely
- **Quarterly priority setting** — choosing 1–2 spheres to focus on each quarter

**Process**:
1. Score current state for each sphere (1–10) + write one honest sentence per sphere (10 min)
2. Identify the **Neglected Sphere** (lowest score) and the **Over-invested Sphere** (highest score) (5 min)
3. Diagnose the **Why** — what is causing the imbalance? (10 min)
   - Time allocation? Competing commitments?
   - Belief or value misalignment? (e.g., "Family is important, but I invest all energy in Karma Bhoomi")
   - Skill gap or fear? (e.g., avoiding difficult conversations in Sangha)
   - Burnout or energy depletion? (Sharira-Prana impacts all spheres)
4. Set **Quarterly Sphere Priority** (1–2 spheres to intentionally increase) (10 min)
5. Identify **Specific Actions per Priority Sphere** (to be tracked in GTD) (10 min)

**Output**:
- Five Spheres current-state assessment
- Identified imbalance root causes
- Quarterly priority (1–2 spheres)
- 3–5 concrete actions per priority sphere → GTD tracking

**Optionality**: 🟢 **Highly recommended** — essential for integrity and balance work

---

### Session 2.3: Svadharma Clarity Session (45–60 min)

**Purpose**: Deepen the mentee's connection to life purpose and calling.

**Agent**: Svadharma Navigator (primary) + Inner Mastery Coach (secondary for shadow work)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Deep purpose questioning** (ICF Competency 7 — Powerful Questions) — questions that open new thinking
- **Saboteur pattern recognition** (Inner Mastery) — what prevents alignment between calling and action
- **Single-idea discernment** — separating the mentee's governing idea from noise, status goals, and borrowed ambition
- **Vision holding** — helping mentees see and trust their calling even when circumstances are difficult

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Svadharma articulation** (JP + Gita) — clarity on life purpose aligned with strength and contribution
- **Calling vs. conditioning awareness** — distinguishing authentic calling from inherited/imposed shoulds
- **One-idea concentration** (Vivekananda) — identifying the governing idea worth organizing life and effort around in this season
- **Purpose-motivation alignment** — understanding what truly drives them vs. what drains them
- **10-year vision embodiment** — not as abstract, but as lived possibility

**Precondition**: Complete Session 2.2 (Five Spheres clarity required before deep purpose work)

**Process**:
Load `agents/svadharma-navigator.md` + `references/jp-philosophical-foundation.md` + `references/management-wisdom-playbook.md` + VIA strengths data

1. **Strengths-to-calling bridge** (10 min) — VIA top 5 strengths → What work uses these?
2. **10-year vision refresh** (10 min) — Move beyond intake answer; what has evolved?
3. **Svadharma articulation** (15 min) — Complete: "My Svadharma is to _____, with _____, so that _____"
4. **One Idea discernment** (10 min) — From this Svadharma, what is the one idea that should organize this season of life? Test it against Vivekananda's standard: is it alive enough to think of, dream of, and align action around?
5. **Calling-to-life-path mapping** (10 min) — If this is the calling, what changes? In role? In community? In daily practice?
6. **Shadow work** (10 min) ⚠️ **Conditional** — If major resistance emerges: "What am I afraid will happen if I fully commit to this calling?"
7. **Three-part commitment** (5 min) — Short-term (this quarter), medium-term (this year), long-term (next 5 years)

**Output**:
- Clear Svadharma articulation
- Named One Idea for this season (or explicit note that it is still emerging)
- Updated 10-year vision
- Calling-aligned actions → GTD
- Identified obstacles and counter-moves

**Optionality**: 🟢 **Highly recommended** — essential for direction and motivation

### Session 2.4: Organizational Attitude Deep Dive (45 min) ⚠️ **Conditional**

**Purpose**: Assess and strengthen leadership capacity across Vivekananda's five OA components.

**Agent**: Organizational Attitude Builder (primary) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (secondary)

**Applicable to**:
- Mentees in formal or informal leadership roles
- Mentees working in teams or institutions
- Mentees with SPG (Adult Social Purpose Group) contributions
- **Not required** for individual professionals or early-career mentees without team responsibilities

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Organizational systems coaching** (Vivekananda framework) — how to build team/institution, not just do personal work
- **Leadership style assessment** — adaptive vs. technical, directive vs. facilitative

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Organizational Attitude mastery** (JP core) — Lakshya Nishtha, Anushasan, Sahakar, Seva Bhava, Sanghatan
- **Team and institutional thinking** — moving from individual to collective
- **Saboteur pattern recognition in groups** — recognizing when the team's energy is reactive
- **Adaptive leadership** (Heifetz) — leading through uncertainty and change

**Precondition**: Session 2.3 (Svadharma clarity) — understood personal purpose before organizational leadership

**Process**:
Load `agents/organizational-attitude-builder.md` + OA Leadership Profile data + `references/LIFE-SKILLS-FRAMEWORK.md` (Component 9: Leadership Influence and Strategic Statecraft) + `references/management-wisdom-playbook.md`

1. **Five OA Components Assessment** (15 min) — Review test data; discuss lived experience
   - Lakshya Nishtha (Goal clarity and commitment): How clear is *your* goal, and how clear is your team's?
   - Anushasan (Self-discipline): What standards do you hold yourself to? What standards does your team?
   - Sahakar (Cooperation): How deeply collaborative is your leadership?
   - Seva Bhava (Service orientation): Is your work fundamentally for others, or for recognition?
   - Sanghatan (Organization-building): What are you building that didn't exist before?

2. **Strongest Component** (5 min) — Where do you lead naturally? How can you strengthen it further?

3. **Development Edge** (15 min) — Which component is weakest? Why? What would strengthen it?
   - (Example: If Sahakar is low, explore: Does mentee believe others can lead? Trust issues? Perfectionism?)

4. **Adaptive Leadership Scenarios** (10 min) ⚠️ **Conditional** — If mentee has current team/organizational challenge:
   - Bring the specific challenge: team misalignment, change resistance, etc.
   - Coach through Heifetz's adaptive leadership lens

5. **Influence Baseline and Levers** (5 min)
   - Rate monthly influence baseline (self-mastery, trust signals, information quality, adaptation speed, incentive alignment, discipline fairness)
   - Select one immediate lever using the ethical sequence: Sama -> Dana -> Bheda -> Danda

6. **Commitments** (5 min) -> GTD

**Output**:
- Five OA components assessment + growth priorities
- Leadership style clarity (adaptive/technical balance)
- Leadership influence baseline + selected leverage move
- One contextual wisdom line translated into one weekly leadership behavior (prefer Gita/Upanishad/Subhashita/Indian idiom first, then Indian saints/philosophers/modern Indian leaders; use modern global voice as optional reinforcement)
- Team/organizational development actions → GTD

**Optionality**: 🟡 **Conditional** (required if mentee has team/SPG leadership; skip otherwise)

### Session 2.5: Inner Mastery & Sthitaprajna Coaching (45 min) ⚠️ **Conditional**

**Purpose**: Develop emotional regulation, Saboteur awareness, and Sthitaprajna (inner equanimity).

**Agent**: Inner Mastery Coach (primary) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (secondary)

**Applicable to**:
- Mentees reporting high stress, reactivity, or emotional dysregulation
- Mentees in intense roles (startup founder, organizational change leader, crisis management)
- Mentees with known anxiety, perfectionism, or imposter syndrome patterns
- Mentees explicitly interested in spiritual/contemplative development
- **Can be skipped** by mentees with stable emotional baseline and low stress

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Emotional intelligence coaching** — helping mentees recognize and regulate their inner state
- **Saboteur pattern mapping** (Positive Intelligence) — identifying the internal critic patterns
- **Crisis/stress protocol** — knowing when to activate support vs. when to coach through

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Sthitaprajna embodiment** (JP/Gita ideal) — equanimity in success and failure, praise and blame
- **Saboteur pattern awareness** — recognizing when the internal critic is running the show
- **Stress physiology understanding** — how nervous system dysregulation drives reactivity
- **Inner resource access** — practices to return to center (pranayama, meditation, grounding)

**Precondition**: Session 2.1 (Morning Protocol established) — inner practices are prerequisite

**Red Flag Indicators** (escalation to mental health professional):
- Suicidal ideation or self-harm
- Undiagnosed/untreated anxiety or depression
- Acute trauma response
- Substance use patterns

**Process**:
Load `agents/inner-mastery-coach.md` + Saboteur profile data (from OA test)

1. **Saboteur Pattern Identification** (10 min) — Which saboteur is most active? (Controller, Judge, Stickler, Avoider, Pleaser, Victim, Impostor)
   - How does it show up in daily life?
   - Under what conditions does it most activate?
   - What is it protecting you from?

2. **Stress Response Audit** (10 min)
   - When you're under stress, how do you typically respond? (Fight/Flight/Freeze)
   - What is the cost of this response?
   - What would the opposite look like?

3. **Inner Resource Discovery** (10 min)
   - When you feel most at peace/resourced, what enables that?
   - What practices have you tried? What works?
   - What could be a daily anchor practice?

4. **Saboteur Shift Practice** (10 min)
   - Name one situation where the saboteur is most active
   - What would Sthitaprajna look like in that situation?
   - What micro-practice could you test this week?

5. **Commitment** (5 min) → GTD

**Output**:
- Named Saboteur pattern(s)
- Stress response physiology understood
- Daily inner resource practice established
- One specific saboteur shift commitment

**Optionality**: 🟡 **Conditional** (recommended if stress/reactivity patterns are evident; optional otherwise)

## Phase 3: ONGOING DEPTH & MASTERY (Months 3+)

### Session 3.1: Weekly Svadhyaya Review (60–90 min, every Sunday or equivalent)

**Purpose**: The central coaching rhythm — reflecting on lived experience, refining commitments, planning the next week.

**Agent**: All six agents (rotates based on what emerges)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Reflective listening** (ICF Competency 6) — tracking patterns across weeks
- **Coaching in the moment** — addressing live challenges as they emerge

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Svadhyaya mastery** (JP core) — honest, rigorous self-study without judgment or rationalization
- **Pattern recognition** — seeing what's working and what's stuck
- **Responsive adaptation** — adjusting sankalpas based on experience, not doubling down on what isn't working

**Process**:
Complete `references/weekly-rhythm.md` → Seven-day Svadhyaya Ritual (all sections)

This is the **central checkpoint** of the coaching system. Every other session flows from and feeds into this.

**Core sections** (in order):
1. **Five Spheres Scan** (15 min) — Score each sphere, one honest sentence per sphere
2. **Sthitaprajna Reflection** (10 min) — Where did I respond with equanimity vs. react?
3. **Dopamine Audit** (5 min) — What drove me this week? Meaning or reactivity?
4. **Organizational Attitude Check** (10 min) — How did I show up in relationships/work?
5. **SPG Pulse** (5 min) — Did I advance my contribution?
6. **Mentor's Mirror** (5 min) — Who did I mentor/learn from?
7. **One Idea Alignment Check** (5 min) — Did this week's actions actually serve the One Idea, or did life fragment into competing agendas?
8. **Next Week's Integral Sankalpa** (15 min) — One commitment per sphere, aligned to the One Idea where it is already clear
9. **Learning Distillation** (10 min) — What is the one insight worth keeping?

**Coaching additions** (beyond solo ritual):
- Coach reads back patterns from past month (2–3 min)
- Coach highlights progress, shifts, growth (3 min)
- Coach asks: "What wants to happen next?" (not what *should* happen)
- Coach asks: "What one idea, if honored this week, would make the rest of your actions more coherent?"
- Coach helps refine Sankalpas for next week (5 min)

**Output**:
- Updated profile with weekly data
- Next week's five Sankalpas (one per sphere)
- One Idea alignment insight captured
- One learning insight captured
- Any new actions → GTD

**Optionality**: 🟢 **Strongly recommended** for ongoing coaching (weekly or bi-weekly minimum)

**Process** (20 min):

3. What one thing would help you most right now? (5 min)
4. Quick plan/reframe (5 min)
5. Commit to one specific action (5 min)

**Output**:
- One course-correction or momentum boost
- One action clarified

**Optionality**: 🟡 **Optional** (useful for mentees managing high complexity or facing obstacles)

### Session 3.3: Monthly Sphere Deep Dive (45 min) ⚠️ **Rotating/Optional**

**Purpose**: Each month, choose one sphere and go deep — diagnose, plan, commit.

**Agents**: Sphere-specific agent + supporting agent(s)

**Pattern**: 
- Month 1: Atma (Inner Mastery Coach) + Svadharma Navigator
- Month 2: Sangha (Integral Life Coach) + Inner Mastery Coach
- Month 3: Karma Bhoomi (Organizational Attitude Builder) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst
- Month 4: Samaj Seva (Organizational Attitude Builder) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst
- Month 5: Sharira-Prana (Integral Life Coach)
- Month 6+: Cycle repeats or rotates based on mentee priorities

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Deep systems coaching** in one domain
- **Root cause analysis** — going beyond symptoms to underlying patterns

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Sphere mastery** — each sphere can become a lifelong practice
- **Deep diagnosis** — understanding what's actually blocking progress
- **Targeted practice** — experiments tailored to the sphere

**Process** (45 min):
1. **State of the sphere** (10 min) — Current score, honest assessment, what's working, what's not
2. **Root cause analysis** (15 min) — Why is this sphere at its current level?
   - Is it values misalignment? Time allocation? Skill gap? Environmental? Beliefs about what's possible?
3. **Vision for this sphere** (5 min) — If this sphere were thriving, what would that look like?
4. **Targeted experiments** (10 min) — What are 2–3 specific things to try this month?
5. **Commit** (5 min) → GTD

**Output**:
- Deep understanding of one sphere's current state and roots
- 2–3 targeted monthly experiments
- Progress metrics for next month

**Optionality**: 🟡 **Optional but recommended** — rotates, not all at once

### Session 3.4: Quarterly Strategic Review (90 min) ⚠️ **Recommended Quarterly**

**Purpose**: Zoom out and assess progress against larger patterns; adjust strategy.

**Agents**: Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (primary) + Svadharma Navigator (secondary)

**Pattern**: End of Quarter (every 3 months)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Systems thinking** (Paristhiti Jnana) — how all the pieces fit together
- **Strategic coaching** — helping mentees stay true to their bigger vision while adapting tactics

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Quarterly reflection practice** — zooming out beyond weekly to see larger patterns
- **Strategy vs. tactics distinction** — what's changing for good reason vs. what's drift?
- **Coherence discernment** — testing whether one governing idea is actually integrating the quarter's choices
- **Cumulative progress recognition** — celebrating how far they've come over 12 weeks

**Process** (90 min):
1. **Five Spheres quarterly assessment** (15 min) — How has each sphere evolved over 3 months?
2. **Organizational Attitude assessment** (10 min) — Are leadership/team behaviors improving?
3. **Progress against Svadharma** (10 min) — Am I more aligned with my calling than 3 months ago?
4. **One Idea coherence review** (10 min) — Is the current One Idea still true, or has it become stale, performative, or too small for what is emerging?
5. **Leadership Influence scorecard** (10 min) — Apply Component 9 monthly scorecard trends from `references/LIFE-SKILLS-FRAMEWORK.md` (self-mastery, trust, information quality, adaptation, incentives, discipline)
6. **Major obstacles identified** (15 min) — What's persistently in the way?
   - Internal (belief, capacity, saboteur)?
   - External (environment, relationships, constraints)?
   - Systemic (multiple spheres creating a knot)?
7. **Learning harvest** (10 min) — What do I know about myself now that I didn't 3 months ago?
8. **Strategy refresh** (15 min) — Do my Sankalpas need adjustment? Is my vision still current? Does the One Idea need sharpening?
9. **Next quarter intentions** (5 min) → GTD

**Output**:
- Quarterly progress assessment
- One Idea confirmed, sharpened, or consciously revised
- Identified systemic patterns
- Leadership influence trend (improving/stable/eroding) with recovery actions if needed
- Adjusted strategy/sankalpas if needed
- Next quarter's focus areas
- **360-degree feedback trigger check**: Has 3+ months passed and the mentee has not yet done a 360? If so, initiate `references/360-feedback-protocol.md` as the next special session.

**Optionality**: 🟢 **Recommended** (every 3 months, essential for sustained progress)

### Session 3.5: Annual Vision Retreat (1–2 days)

**Purpose**: The deepest, most comprehensive recalibration — who am I becoming, am I on track, what needs to shift?

**Agents**: All six agents; coach offers continuity across the retreat

**Pattern**: Once per year (typically Dec-Jan or on personal new year/birthday)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Deep systems facilitation** — holding space for profound reflection and integration
- **Whole-life coaching** — helping mentee see all the way back to who they're becoming

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Annual Svadhyaya mastery** (JP core) — the most rigorous self-study of the year
- **Life narrative coherence** — seeing their life as a continuous arc, not fragmented years
- **Shadow work** (Immunity to Change) — honest reckoning with what's blocking them
- **Recommitment to purpose** — active choice to continue on this path, not default

**Process**:
Complete `references/yearly-strategy.md` → Full Annual Vision Retreat protocol (1–2 days)

**Day 1: The Honest Retrospective**
- Five Spheres year review (30 min)
- Organizational Attitude year review (20 min)
- Sthitaprajna self-assessment (20 min)
- Relationships inventory (20 min)

- Shadow work / Immunity to Change (30 min)

**Day 2: The Visioning**
- 10-year vision refresh (30 min)
- Svadharma recalibration (30 min)
- SPG progress assessment (20 min)
- Letter to yourself in 5 years (30 min)
- Next year's Integral Sankalpa (5 per sphere) (30 min)

**Coaching additions**:
- Coach reads back themes and patterns from the year (20 min)
- Coach highlights shadow work insights; helps mentee sit with discomfort/truth (30 min)

**Output**:

## Phase 4: ADVANCED & SPECIALIZED COACHING

### Session 4.1: Leadership Challenge Coaching (60 min) ⚠️ **As-needed**

**Purpose**: When a mentee faces a specific, acute leadership or organizational challenge.

**Agents**: Organizational Attitude Builder (primary) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (secondary)

**Triggers**:
- Team conflict or misalignment
- Change management challenge
- Organizational culture issue
- Difficult stakeholder relationship
- Organizational Attitude component breakdown

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Adaptive leadership coaching** (Heifetz) — coaching through ambiguity and technical + adaptive challenges
- **Organizational systems thinking** — understanding the challenge as a system, not a person problem

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Adaptive leadership** — leading through uncertainty without knowing the answer
- **Multi-perspective thinking** — seeing the challenge from stakeholder perspectives
- **Intervention design** — planning moves that address root causes, not just symptoms

**Process** (60 min):
1. **Situation framing** (15 min) — What is the challenge exactly? Who is affected? What's at stake?
2. **Root cause diagnosis** (15 min) — Technical problem (fixable) or Adaptive challenge (requires learning and change)?
3. **Perspective mapping** (10 min) — How do different stakeholders see this?
4. **OA + Influence lens analysis** (10 min) — Which Organizational Attitude component(s) and influence pillar(s) need strengthening?
   - Use Component 9 from `references/LIFE-SKILLS-FRAMEWORK.md`: self-mastery, information quality, adaptive positioning, incentives/discipline
5. **Intervention planning** (10 min) — What moves will you make? What will you try?
   - Choose one influence lever sequence: Sama -> Dana -> Bheda -> Danda
   - If influence is eroding, trigger 30-day influence recovery sprint

**Output**:
- Clear diagnosis of the challenge
- Planned interventions
- Stakeholder strategy
- Influence lever plan with timing and boundary conditions
- One management wisdom anchor tied to action (default to Gita/Subhashita/Upanishad/Indian idiom, then Indian saints/philosophers/modern Indian leaders; use modern global insight as secondary support)
- One specific action to try this week

**Optionality**: 🟡 **As-needed** (triggered by real-time challenge)

### Session 4.2: Purpose Recalibration / Svadharma Pivot (60–90 min) ⚠️ **As-needed**

**Purpose**: When a mentee's life direction shifts — new role, family change, loss, new calling — and Svadharma needs rethinking.

**Agents**: Svadharma Navigator (primary) + Inner Mastery Coach (secondary) + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst (for context)

**Triggers**:
- Job change or exit
- Family transition (marriage, children, empty nest)
- Loss or major challenge
- Life stage shift (early career → mid-career, professional → elder)
- Calling shift (felt sense that old direction no longer fits)

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Transitions coaching** — helping mentees navigate identity and meaning shifts
- **Discernment** — distinguishing true calling from fear-based reaction
- **Integration** — helping mentees carry forward what was valuable before

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Pivot without abandonment** — honoring past commitments while moving forward
- **Discernment practice** — deeper clarity on what's true calling vs. conditioning
- **Idea renewal** — recognizing when the old organizing idea has completed its work and a new one must be named
- **New chapter beginning** — stepping into new identity with intention

**Process** (60–90 min):
1. **What is changing and why?** (15 min) — Describe the external shift and your inner response
2. **Grieve the past** (10 min) — What are you letting go of? What was valuable about what's ending?
3. **New possibilities** (15 min) — In this new context, what wants to emerge? What feels true?
4. **Svadharma rearticulation** (15 min) — How does your calling evolve with this new chapter?
5. **One Idea reassessment** (10 min) — Does the previous One Idea still hold, or is a new governing idea now asking for full commitment?
6. **Five Spheres re-calibration** (15 min) — How will sphere allocation shift?
7. **Commitment to the new chapter** (10 min) — What are you saying "yes" to? What are you saying "no" to?

**Output**:
- Grief work and closure on the previous chapter
- Rearticulated Svadharma (if shifted)
- One Idea retained, revised, or renamed for the new chapter
- New Five Sphere allocation
- Commitments to the new chapter

**Optionality**: 🟡 **As-needed** (triggered by major life transition)

### Session 4.3: Depth Coaching in a Specialized Domain ⚠️ **Optional/Advanced**

**Purpose**: Extended coaching in one specialized area — building mastery in a single domain over 6–12 months of recurring monthly sessions.

**Agents**: Domain-specialist agent(s) as listed per track below

**Applicable to**:
- Mentees who want to go deeper in one domain (e.g., "I want to master organizational building")
- Mentees at advanced life stage with stable foundations in Phase 2 work
- Mentees with complex professional/social roles demanding specialized depth

**Precondition**: All Phase 2 sessions complete; mentee has a stable Morning Protocol and Weekly Review rhythm.

**Skills Developed** *(Coach)*:
- **Domain mastery facilitation** — sustained coaching over months in a single area, tracking cumulative growth
- **Plateau-breaking technique** — recognizing when a mentee has plateaued and designing breakthrough interventions
- **Teaching coaching** — as mentees advance, coaching shifts toward helping them coach others

**Skills Developed** *(Mentee)*:
- **Domain depth** — mastery in one chosen area (Organizational Attitude, Sthitaprajna, SPG strategy, or leadership influence)
- **Articulating what you've learned** — the ability to teach a domain signals true mastery
- **Identifying next growing edge** — within a domain, the mastery frontier keeps receding

**Tracks** (choose one; run at monthly 60-min cadence):

**Track A: Organizational Attitude Mastery**
*Agent*: Organizational Attitude Builder + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst
*Process per session*:
1. OA component score update (10 min) — which component moved and why?
2. One deep OA challenge explored (20 min) — team, institution, or SPG challenge through OA lens
3. Influence lever application review (15 min) — what happened when you used the lever selected last session?
4. Next lever/experiment committed (10 min) → GTD
5. One management wisdom anchor (5 min) — select from `references/management-wisdom-playbook.md`; prefer Indian-origin; convert to one behavioral commitment

**Track B: Sthitaprajna Mastery**
*Agent*: Inner Mastery Coach + Svadharma Navigator
*Process per session*:
1. Inner state since last session (10 min) — what destabilized you? What held?
2. Saboteur evolution check (10 min) — is the primary saboteur shifting? What new one is appearing?
3. Deep Gita or Upanishad inquiry (15 min) — explore one verse through lived experience, not theory
4. Contemplative practice review and refinement (15 min) — how is the Sadhana architecture evolving?
5. Committed inner experiment for next month (10 min)

**Track C: SPG Co-Creation and Leadership**
*Agent*: Organizational Attitude Builder + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst
*Process per session*:
1. SPG progress update (10 min) — what was built, what stalled?
2. Systemic analysis of SPG context (15 min) — Layer 1–4 Paristhiti reading of current SPG environment
3. Team and coalition-building work (15 min) — who needs to be brought in? Who is a blocker?
4. Strategy refinement (15 min) — is the SPG theory of change still current?
5. Commitments (5 min) → GTD

**Track D: Strategic Influence Mastery**
*Agent*: Organizational Attitude Builder + Paristhiti Jnana Analyst + Inner Mastery Coach (for inner state)
*Process per session*:
Using Component 9 from `references/LIFE-SKILLS-FRAMEWORK.md` (Leadership Influence and Strategic Statecraft):
1. Monthly influence scorecard review (10 min) — self-mastery, trust, information quality, adaptation, incentives, discipline: each scored 1–5
2. The month's hardest influence challenge (15 min) — what was at stake? What happened?
3. Lever sequencing analysis (15 min) — which levers (Sama, Dana, Bheda, Danda) were used? Were they appropriate?
4. Statecraft reflection (10 min) — what would a Sthitaprajna leader have done differently?
5. Next month's influence experiment (10 min) → GTD

**Output**:
- Domain-specific cumulative growth log (maintained in profile under "Depth Track Progress")
- Monthly commitments → GTD
- At 6-month mark: articulate what you've learned in language simple enough to teach another

**Optionality**: 🟡 **Optional/Advanced** (for mentees choosing specialized depth; one track at a time)

## Integration with GTD & Daily Practice

### How Coaching Sessions Feed into GTD

Each coaching session generates **Coached Decisions** (CoD) — actions the mentee is committing to.

1. **During session**: Coach identifies 1–3 key commitments emerging
2. **In GTD**: Entry format: `[Commitment from Session X] @context [Session date]`
3. **Tracking**: Reviewed in next Weekly Review; progressed through GTD steps
4. **Accountability**: Reflected on in next coaching session


## Escalation & When to Refer

### When to Recommend Professional Support

**Mental Health / Crisis**:
- Active suicidal ideation → Refer to crisis line immediately
- Undiagnosed/untreated depression, anxiety → Refer to therapist
- Trauma response → Refer to trauma-specialized therapist
- Substance use pattern → Refer to addiction specialist

**Professional / Organizational**:
- Complex legal/HR issue → Refer to employment lawyer
- Performance issues in role → Refer to specialist coach or mentor in that domain
- Unresolved family trauma → Refer to family therapist

**Spiritual / Philosophical**:
- Deep spiritual crisis → Refer to spiritual director or elder from JP tradition
- Existential questions beyond coaching scope → Refer to philosopher or contemplative practitioner

## Conclusion: The Coaching Cadence

The Hitaishin coaching system works best as a **recurring practice**, not occasional intervention.

**Minimum Effective Rhythm**:
- Weekly (or bi-weekly) Sunday Review sessions — the heartbeat
- Quarterly strategic review — the compass check
- Annual retreat — the recalibration
- Ad-hoc specialized sessions as needed

**This rhythm** — daily practice, weekly reflection, quarterly strategy, annual vision — creates the conditions for genuine transformation. Each layer reinforces the others.

*The goal is not perfect adherence to a plan, but a mentee who increasingly knows themselves, trusts their Svadharma, and shows up with full presence and integrity in all Five Spheres.*
